## III. Niche Terminology & Technical Definitions

1. **Nature Aquarium** – Amano’s aesthetic philosophy emphasizing balanced hardscape (wood/stone), plant grouping, and negative space to replicate natural landscapes, requiring precise CO₂/light/nutrient synergy.  
2. **Aqua Soil** – Nutrient-rich volcanic substrate (e.g., ADA Amazonia) that buffers pH to 6.0-6.8 and provides root fertilization, critical for carpet plants in high-tech setups.  
3. **Golden Ratio** – 1:1.61 composition rule where key hardscape elements divide the tank into harmonious zones, adapted here for functional flow (e.g., fish pathways, nutrient distribution).  
4. **Biotope** – A replicated micro-environment mimicking a specific geographic region’s water chemistry, flora, and fauna (e.g., Amazon blackwater vs. African rift lake).  
5. **Zero-Water-Change System** – Advanced filtration relying on microbial mats, aquatic plants, and careful bioload management to minimize water replacement, ideal for sustainability-focused setups.  
6. **ORP (Oxidation-Reduction Potential)** – Water’s electron activity measured in mV; below 250 mV indicates reducing conditions (beneficial for plants), above 350 mV promotes algae.  
7. **CO₂ Diffusion Efficiency** – Ratio of dissolved CO₂ vs. injected CO₂, optimized via reactor design (e.g., inline vs. atomizing) to maximize plant uptake and minimize waste.  
8. **Aquascape "Rule of Thirds"** – Layout principle dividing the tank into foreground/midground/background zones with focal points placed at intersections, adapted for functional plant growth tiers.
