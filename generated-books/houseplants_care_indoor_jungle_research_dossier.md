## III. Niche Terminology & Technical Definitions

To establish absolute authority and provide readers with a robust foundational understanding, the ebook will define key terminology essential for informed houseplant care and design.

1.  **Biophilic Design:** An architectural and interior design concept that incorporates natural elements and processes into built environments, aiming to connect occupants with nature. In houseplant care, it refers to creating spaces that enhance human well-being through the presence of plants, natural light, and organic forms.
2.  **Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR):** A financial metric representing the mean annual growth rate of an investment over a specified period longer than one year, assuming the profits are reinvested. In market analysis, it's used to smooth out volatility and show steady growth trends (e.g., for the indoor plant market).
3.  **Direct-to-Consumer (DTC):** A business model where a company sells its products directly to customers, bypassing traditional third-party retailers or wholesalers. This model, exemplified by The Sill and Bloomscape, allows for greater control over brand experience, pricing, and customer relationships.
4.  **Subirrigation:** A watering method where plants absorb water from a reservoir below the pot, rather than being watered from above. This system, often used in self-watering planters or large commercial installations, allows plants to take up water as needed, promoting consistent moisture and reducing overwatering risks.
5.  **Plant Physiology:** The scientific study of the vital functions of plants, including processes such as photosynthesis, respiration, nutrient uptake, water transport, and growth. Understanding plant physiology is crucial for intuitive plant care, as it explains *why* plants react to environmental stimuli in specific ways.
6.  **Microclimate:** The local atmospheric conditions differing from those of the surrounding area, particularly within a small, confined space. In houseplant care, a home's microclimates (e.g., near a window, in a humid bathroom, under an air vent) significantly impact a plant's specific light, humidity, and temperature needs.
7.  **Urban Jungle:** An aesthetic and lifestyle trend characterized by filling indoor spaces, particularly in urban environments, with a dense and diverse collection of houseplants, creating a lush, immersive, and often therapeutic green environment.
8.  **Evidence-Based Practices:** Care methods and recommendations that are supported by scientific research, botanical principles, and observable results, rather than relying on anecdotal advice, traditional myths, or unverified claims. This approach underpins the "Unconventional Wisdom" aspect of our ebook.
